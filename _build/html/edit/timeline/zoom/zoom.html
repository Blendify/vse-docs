2.1.2. Zoom¶

In a video project it is important that you can zoom in on the section you’re working on. On the other hand it’s also important to have an overview of your timeline. Zooming in and out is thus a frequent occurring action.

Home: zooms in on the total project; from start to finish. When you ever get lost in your timeline, press the Home key to get the complete picture. With the menu: View > Frame All.

NumpadPeriod: Zooms in the display to fit only the selected strips. Menu: View > Frame Selected.

Shift-B: Click and drag to draw a rectangle and zoom to this rectangle.

Wheel: Scrolling the middle mouse wheel will zoom in on the section where the cursor is.

Ctrl-MMB and drag left/right or drag on the circles on the horizontal scrollbar.

You can also zoom in vertically; make the strip bars wider or narrower. Use Shift-Wheel, or drag on the circles of the left scrollbar. Or: Ctrl-MMB and drag up/down
